# High Quality Insight Examples

## Example 1

### Input pattern
- paycheck every other Friday
- dining spikes within 72 hours after paycheck
- savings transfer happens 5 days later
- account balance drops sharply by mid-cycle

### Good insight
Title:
Payday spending is eating into your intended savings window

Summary:
Your discretionary spending jumps right after income arrives, before your savings transfer has time to lock in progress.

Behavioral interpretation:
This suggests your highest-risk spending period is not random. It is clustered around the moment you feel flush.

Recommendation:
Move your savings transfer to the same day income arrives, or within 24 hours.

Why this is good
- specific
- tied to timing
- behavioral but not overreaching
- actionable

## Example 2

### Input pattern
- grocery spend stable
- delivery app spend highly variable
- weeks with 3+ delivery orders correlate with higher total food spend

### Good insight
Title:
Food overspending is coming from convenience, not groceries

Summary:
Your grocery costs are steady. The real swing factor is delivery frequency.

Behavioral interpretation:
The issue is likely decision friction and convenience spending, not food inflation alone.

Recommendation:
Set a weekly delivery cap or pre-plan two fallback meals.

Why this is good
- isolates the real driver
- avoids obvious budgeting talk
- points to behavior change

## Example 3

### Input pattern
- quarterly school-related costs
- repeated spikes every 3 months
- user labels them as unexpected

### Good insight
Title:
Some of your “surprise” costs are actually predictable

Summary:
Several large education-related expenses recur quarterly, which means they are irregular but not truly unexpected.

Behavioral interpretation:
Your budget may be treating recurring seasonal obligations like emergencies.

Recommendation:
Create a quarterly sinking fund category and contribute monthly.

Why this is good
- reframes the issue
- turns noise into pattern
- leads to strategy
