# Insight Rubric

A strong insight must meet most or all of the following:

## 1. Specific
Bad:
- You spend a lot on food.

Good:
- Restaurant spending is concentrated in the first four days after payday.

## 2. Numerically grounded
Bad:
- Your spending seems inconsistent.

Good:
- Weekly discretionary spending varied by 42% over the last 8 weeks.

## 3. Non-obvious
Bad:
- You have subscriptions.

Good:
- You have 3 small recurring charges that together exceed your largest streaming subscription.

## 4. Personalized
Bad:
- People often overspend on weekends.

Good:
- Your weekend spending is 2.3x your weekday average, mostly driven by dining and convenience purchases.

## 5. Actionable
Bad:
- You may want to be careful.

Good:
- Moving one annual charge into a monthly sinking fund would smooth cash flow during high-expense months.

## 6. Honest about confidence
Bad:
- This clearly means you are impulsive.

Good:
- This may indicate reactive spending around pay periods, but confidence is moderate because the pattern is based on only 2 months of data.

## Reject weak insights
Reject anything that is:
- generic
- obvious
- unsupported
- repetitive
- moralizing
- fake-psychological
